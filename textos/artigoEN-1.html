Aristocracy in Child of the Dark

May 3, 2026

During the government of then-governor of the state of São Paulo, Jânio Quadros, the author describes how, in a daily life of misery, neighbors all equally poor often turn hostile toward one another, in a scenario where “the poor hate the poor.” The book shows what we could call horizontal rivalry, people who, in the words of Paulo Freire in Pedagogy of the Oppressed: “The oppressed, instead of directing their struggle against the one who oppresses them, assume the values and behaviors of the oppressor.” This can generate hostility against their equals, that is, the working class, of which Carolina, as a paper collector, was part. This class, then, is divided into segments that compete with one another, weakening their collective strength.

Carolina demonstrates resentment toward her neighbors, especially regarding the use of alcohol, fights, and nudity in her community. Different from the representation she later receives throughout the book as a strong and combative woman, she was originally submissive and passive in the face of it all. This prevents her from becoming an aristocrat in Nietzsche’s sense. Resentment is born from impotence, inverting the values of strength and weakness. The Nietzschean aristocrat, on the other hand, does not react to the world with denial or envy: he affirms life itself.

In 1960, Carolina’s notebooks were organized and edited, with changes that reinforced her image as a strong and hardworking woman, better fitting the profile that the publishing market desired. This stance becomes even more present in her book The Brick House. In the book, one can find memorable phrases such as: “In our country, everything is weakening. Money is weak. Democracy is weak and politicians are extremely weak. And everything that is weak will die one day.” This phrase foreshadows the military coup that would take place in the following years.

Another striking passage is: “I even think Black people’s hair is more well-behaved than white people’s hair. Because Black hair, wherever you put it, it stays. It is obedient. And white people’s hair, just shake your head and it goes out of place. It is undisciplined. If reincarnations do exist, I want to always come back Black.”
